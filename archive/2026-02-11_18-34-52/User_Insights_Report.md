# User Insights Report (Customer 面向)

## 1. Jobs to be Done (JTBD) Analysis

-   **Functional Jobs**:
    *   Find fragrances with layered notes and high longevity (6-8 hours).
    *   Minimize per-use costs through deep market research and price comparison.
    *   Avoid purchasing regrets or "brand premium" traps.
-   **Emotional Jobs**:
    *   Feel smart and "in control" of life management.
    *   Gain a sense of self-reward for professional hard work.
    *   Reduce financial guilt associated with luxury consumption.
-   **Social Jobs**:
    *   Be perceived by colleagues and friends as a person of taste and detail.
    *   Communicate an image of being non-sloppy and self-disciplined.

**JTBD Statement**:
"When I am managing my professional lifestyle, I want to find high-quality fragrances at the most rational cost, so I can feel like a capable, smart achiever and project a dignified, tasteful image to my social circle."

## 3. Customer Psychographics & Persona

-   **Who they are**: **The Smart & Elegant Striver (精明優雅的奮鬥者)**. Female professionals (20-40) in urban areas. They are budget-conscious but quality-obsessed. They view consumption as a strategic investment in their self-image and professional trajectory.
-   **Expected Archetype**: **The Sage (智者)**. They don't want to be "sold to" with fluff; they want a mentor who provides data, transparency, and "truth" to help them make wiser decisions. They expect the brand to be their "intellectual companion."
